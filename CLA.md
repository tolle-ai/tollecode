# Contributor License Agreement

## Why this exists

TolleCode is released under the AGPL-3.0. The same code also forms the basis of
commercial Tollecode products that are not AGPL-licensed.

Tollecode can do that today only because it owns the copyright in all of the code.
The moment a contribution from someone else is merged, that contributor owns the
copyright in their patch, and it reaches Tollecode under the AGPL — which would
prohibit shipping it in a commercially licensed build.

This agreement resolves that by granting Tollecode broader rights than the AGPL alone
provides. It is the same arrangement used by most open-core projects.

## How to sign

**Nothing to sign yet — the project isn't accepting pull requests.** This agreement is
published in advance so you can read it before deciding to invest time in a
contribution later.

Once contributions open, a bot will comment on your first pull request with a signing
link. Signing is a one-time step covering all your future contributions.

If you are contributing on behalf of a company, or your employment contract assigns
your copyrights to your employer, your employer must sign a Corporate CLA instead.
Open an issue and we'll arrange it.

## Contributions we can accept without a CLA

Changes that carry no copyrightable expression — typo fixes, whitespace, dependency
version bumps — don't require a signature. If in doubt, sign; it's faster than debating
the threshold.
